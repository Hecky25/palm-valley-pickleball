About PVPA

Our Mission

The Palm Valley Pickleball Association exists to grow the sport of pickleball in our community — creating a welcoming, competitive, and fun environment for players at every stage of their journey.

Our History

Founded by a group of passionate local players, PVPA has grown from a handful of regulars to a thriving community of hundreds. We maintain 6 dedicated courts and run structured play year-round.

Skill Levels

We organize play by skill rating so everyone gets competitive matches. From complete beginners (2.0) to seasoned competitors (4.5+), there's a time slot for every level on our courts.

Get Involved

Joining is simple — just show up during open play hours. We also welcome volunteers, sponsors, and anyone interested in helping grow pickleball in Palm Valley.

Open Play Rotations
Two Up / Two Down Rotation (7 or fewer paddles waiting)
  • Winners remain on the court and split
  • Two new players from the rack join the new game
  • After two consecutive games, leave the court and paddle up again
Groups of four wanting to play together: find the next open color with four available slots and place paddles there. If a group of two or three is ahead, they may pick up a player from those coming off to make four. The foursome plays next.
Four On / Four Off Rotation (8 or more paddles waiting)
  • All four players rotate off after each game
  • Next four players in the queue take the court
Challenge Court

Challenge Courts are a fun way to boost the competitive level of games during Open Play — giving players opportunities to grow their skills by challenging better players and allowing stronger players to share their knowledge. Challenge Courts attract upper-level players and promote a culture of quality play and improvement.

  • Winners stay for up to three games when two or more teams (or four players) are waiting
  • Teams of two are recommended — single players can disrupt the order of play
  • Designed to enable two-person teams to play competitive games
Singles Play
  • Singles play is available if there are open courts
  • If paddles are in the queue, Open Play rotations should be followed
Level of Play
  • Find the colored pickleball sign that matches your level and paddle up
  • Colored signs may change based on who shows up — check before paddling up
  • For your safety and others, assess the level of play on all courts before joining
  • If a Court Ambassador is on duty, ask them to help you find the right court
PVPA Court Etiquette
  • No pets inside courts
  • Shirts and sneakers required
  • No bikes or skateboards on the court
  • No spectators on the courts during play
  • No profanity
  • Play permitted between 8:00 AM and 9:00 PM only
  • Respect all skill-level designations — play where your game belongs
  • Welcome new players and help grow the game
